About Me

Seoyoon Kang is an interior designer whose work explores how space shapes emotion and experience.

I view interior design as a practice that shapes emotion and experience through space. I believe space has the ability to quietly influence how people feel while they inhabit it.

My work begins at the balance between unfamiliarity and comfort. Through careful use of color and detail, I aim to create warm environments where people can release tension and feel at ease.

Selected work

Featured Projects

An adaptive reuse cultural center in Tempe that connects Korean heritage with contemporary community life through exhibition, gathering, craft, and shared experiences.

Overview

This adaptive reuse project transforms a 1967 building in Tempe into the Korean Cultural Center. Organized into three zones, the design guides visitors from introduction to hands-on engagement through gathering, craft, and shared experiences.

Design Direction

Inspired by traditional Korean spatial concepts and dancheong colors, the project bridges heritage and contemporary community life. Spaces include the Madang-Han and Won buildings, Madang, gift shop, exhibition space, information desk, library, craft and cooking studios, dining area, game play zone, lounge, courtyard, tea cafe, and terrace.

A memory care facility designed around aging in place, intuitive wayfinding, nature connection, and a comfortable residential environment for residents with dementia.

Overview

Desert Marigold is grounded in the principles of aging in place, creating an environment that feels comfortable and secure like home. The design uses clear circulation and visual cues to reduce confusion and support independence.

Design Direction

A strong connection to nature supports safety, dignity, and emotional well-being. Features include restaurant and personal patios, communal gardens, a wayfinding path, green roof, outdoor seating, pod-specific flowers, community building, medical and gym areas, kitchen, visiting lounge, restaurant, living pods, bedroom, dining area, corridor, and living room.

A mixed-use family community center that combines coworking, learning, cultural spaces, play, and multifamily housing into one connected environment.

Overview

The Mesquite Project supports families and the local community, allowing parents to work while staying connected to their children. Lower levels integrate coworking, interactive learning, and cultural spaces, with multifamily housing above.

Design Direction

The project proposes a family-centered model where care, work, culture, and living coexist. The Treewalk connects Mesquite After School, music library, art incubator, leasing office, library, coworking space, farm-to-table eatery, clubhouse and gym, circulation, playground, and entrance.
