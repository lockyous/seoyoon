# Project 01: Korean Cultural Center

## Basic Info
Title: Korean Cultural Center  
Location: Tempe, AZ  
Type: Cultural Center  
Software: Revit, SketchUp, Enscape, Photoshop  
Collaboration: Individual  

## Short Card Description
An adaptive reuse cultural center in Tempe that connects Korean heritage with contemporary community life through exhibition, gathering, craft, and shared experiences.

## Full Description
This adaptive reuse project transforms a 1967 building in Tempe into the Korean Cultural Center, creating a space where culture and daily life connect. Organized into three zones, the design guides visitors from introduction to hands-on engagement through gathering, craft, and shared experiences. Inspired by traditional Korean spatial concepts and dancheong colors, the project bridges heritage and contemporary community life.

## Key Design Themes
- Adaptive reuse
- Korean cultural identity
- Community gathering
- Exhibition and craft experience
- Hanok-inspired spatial and structural concepts
- Dancheong-inspired color palette
- Warm materiality and cultural storytelling

# Project 02: Desert Marigold

## Basic Info
Title: Desert Marigold  
Location: Scottsdale, AZ  
Type: Memory Care Facility  
Software: Revit, SketchUp, Enscape, Photoshop  
Collaboration: Team Project with Callie Evarts and Megi Talavia  

## Short Card Description
A memory care facility designed around aging in place, intuitive wayfinding, nature connection, and a comfortable residential environment for residents with dementia.

## Full Description
Desert Marigold is a memory care facility grounded in the principles of aging in place, creating an environment that feels comfortable and secure like home. The design emphasizes intuitive wayfinding through clear circulation and visual cues to reduce confusion and support independence for residents with dementia. By fostering a strong connection to nature, the project promotes safety, dignity, and emotional well-being.

# Project 03: The Mesquite Project

## Basic Info
Title: The Mesquite Project  
Location: Phoenix, AZ  
Type: Family Community Center  
Software: Revit, Enscape, Photoshop  
Collaboration: Individual  

## Short Card Description
A mixed-use family community center that combines coworking, learning, cultural spaces, play, and multifamily housing into one connected environment.

## Full Description
The Mesquite Project is a mixed-use space designed to support families and the local community, allowing parents to work while staying connected to their children. Lower levels integrate coworking, interactive learning, and cultural spaces, while multifamily housing above extends this shared framework. The project proposes a family-centered model where care, work, culture, and living coexist within one cohesive environment.

## Key Design Themes

- Mixed-use development
- Parent-child connection
- Coworking and learning
- Cultural spaces
- Multifamily residential
- Care, work, culture, and living
